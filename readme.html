Introduction

 Seek & Dread Online is a free top-down multiplayer shooter. It is similar to Counter-Strike but it is a 2D-game. You control a terrorist or a police man. You can move your character with the keyboard and aim with the mouse. In the deathmatch mode you have no friends, all that counts is that you survive. Collect firearms and grenades that appear randomly on the playfield and do not forget to refresh your health while you are in the action of heavy gunfights. In the team mode you can choose if you want to join the terrorists or the police forces. Then you can buy your equipment and start to conquer the enemy's base with your team. Planting C4 to destroy walls, flashing your enemies with flashbangs, blowing up your enemies with HE-grenades or corner them with gas grenades will help you to accomplish your mission. Due to the map editor you can create your own scenarios.

Console commands

 Press Enter in game for chatmode (all commands start with "/"). There you have the following options:

User commands

/timeleftdisplays how much time is left until map changes
/nextmapdisplays the next map of the map cycle
//(message)sends a message only to your team(team mode)
/join policeplayer changes to police forces(team mode)
/join terroristsplayer changes to terrorists(team mode)
!Important! Only change team when you are dead or have full health otherwise it counts as suicide.

Admin commands

/resettimeresets time of the map cycle
/kick (player-id)kicks player with that id
/ban (player-id)bans the IP of the player with that id*
/map (map-name)map change
/msg (message)sends a noticeable message to all players
/restartrestarts the current round and resets game score, weapons, money, time (lts mode only)
/closeconthe server does not accept new connections, this cannot be activated again
*the IP is banned until you close the server

Connection and Options

 At the start of the game you need to enter your name. The name must have at least five characters and can only have up to fifteen characters. You can choose from A to Z and 1 to 9. You may also use these special chraracters: -!$+*_'#[]/\|. To Connect click on the connect button. After you connected you will find yourself in the lobby and there you have different options to choose from.

Options

ConnectAfter left clicking on a server this allows you to connect to the server. You can also double click on a server to connect to it.
Connect to IPHere you can join a server by entering the IP and port of the server.
Get Server ListUse this to update the server list so it's more accurate. However, the amount of players in a server will only update every five minutes.
OptionsThis is where you can set your options for the game.
  • Controls (Change default controls but not mouse)
  • Music (On/Off)
  • Change start weapon (SIGP 228 or GLOCK 19C )

  • Footsteps (On/Off)

Exit GameWill make you exit the SND Program.

Hosting Solutions

 Once your hosted check to see if anyone can join your server. If they tell you that they can't then your going to need to try certain methods to fix the problem.

Firewall

  • Open your firewall.
  • Go to where it says Ports.
  • Click on open ports.
  • Write ports 80, 1203 and 1204 TCP. With some firewalls just browse for the SND Program and tell it not to block it.

Router

If you're using a router with the default configuration you wont be able to host games so you need to forward or "open" the port of the game. (Snd uses ports 1203 and 1204 on default and as protocol TCP ) Here is a To Do description for relative new DLINK-Routers, for other routers it might be similar:
  • Open your web browser and enter the IP address of your router (192.168.0.1). Enter username (admin) and your password. Call your service provider to get the router if this does not connect.
  • Click on Advanced and then click Virtual Server.
  • Create a new entry:
  • Click Enabled.
  • Enter a name (Snd).
  • Private IP - Enter the IP address of the computer you want to host the game.
  • Select TCP for Protocol Type
  • Enter 1203 and 1204 for both Private Port and Public Port
  • Click Always or set a schedule.
  • Click Apply and then Continue.

Map cycle

 To activate the map cycle check the box in the host menu. To edit it open the file mapcycle.txt with a text editor. The first line is the time in minutes which will be spend on every map. After that line you can add maps to the map cycle and seperate them with break line. The last line must be empty. Look at all your maps that you have and place the map's name on one of the lines. Do not use the file extention *.sdo. If the name is "lts_bunkers.sdo" then name it "lts_bunkers". If you place in a map you do not have it will ignore it and move to the next line or back to the begining again. The first line must be a map you have. If you do not have that map or typed it wrong you will basicly get a black screen. Map Cycleing is a good way to have players play on different maps and see who can score the most within the map time. Once the map is changed all scores and teams are reseted.

Playing

 When hosting or playing on someone's server there are a few things you should know about. There are three types of games first of all. DM - Deathmatch, TDM - Team-Deathmatch and LTS - Last Team Standing. Information about these games are covered later on. On the top left it shows "1. Weapon" and "2. Weapon". The 1 is for the default weapon "SIGP 228" or "GLOCK 19C". The 2 is for the secondary weapon. The second weapon you will have to pick up or buy. These weapons have a limited amount of ammo. The current weapon you are useing will be highlighted in blue. When you run out of ammo the gun will automatically reload if you got enough ammo otherwise switch your weapons. At the top of the screen your current ammo will be shown for the current weapon you are using. Below that there is a health bar. In TDM and LTS games on the top right it will show the scores for police and terrorists. The bottom left corner will display the ingame chat. There are two shooting modes: red (normal mode) and blue (headshot mode). Headshot mode makes 3x more damage but you can only hit their heads. By pressing the tab key you can see all the players in the game, IDs, scores, and who's currently in the lead.

Deathmatch (DM)

 A Deathmatch game is a type of game where you just kill anyone that you can. This is a good type of game for newbies to start with since it does not take much skill. You get to learn about the different weapons. While playing on this map crates, grenades and health packs will appear randomly on the playfield. The crates contain random weapons. However, if a host blocked certain weapons then it will not appear in these crates. Health packs will heal you by 40 hitpoints. If you pick up a grenade it will either be a "Gas Grenade" or a "HE Grenade". If you die then you have to wait five seconds. Then when your ready to fight again you need to press the "Space Bar".

Team-Deathmatch (DM)

 A Team-Deathmatch game is exactly like a Deathmatch except it's played with teams. This is good for newbies who want to try to play on a team. If your on the police side you need to kill a terrorist. If your on the terrorist side you need to kill the police. If you kill one of your own teammate you will lose two points from your score. The more you kill your own teammates the bigger the chance to get banned. So this will help your team playing skills.

Last Team Standing (LTS)

 Last Team Standing is where a lot of skilled players play. As a newbie your free to play it but you should at least be skilled enough to not kill your teammates. There are two teams, Police and Terrorists. In a TDM game you get the same weapons for either side. In a LTS game this is true except for "Flashbang" and "HE Grenades". In a LTS game the round does not end until there is only one team standing. This is another reason to be a bit skilled, otherwise you might get bored of waiting for the next round. In this type of game you buy your weapons, grenades and ammo at the begining of the round. You start with $1,500 and each round you get the same amount. If you manage to kill someone you get a $500 bonus for each player you kill. If you kill your own teammate you lose all your money and two points. If you die in a round you just lose your weapon.

Tips for newbies

 As a newbie you might have some problems killing, aiming or just need some advice. This section is to give you a few tips to help you improve your skills. Here are ten tips to help you play better.

Top 10 Tips

  • Using headshots can only be used to aim at their heads and kill them faster. However it's harder to kill when moving. So use normal mode when they are low or you are missing a lot.
  • Know your weapons. Learn to recognize what sound each weapon makes- it's (almost) the only way you can tell what guns others have. Also know how each weapon works and its strenghts and weaknesses (ex. usually you shouldnt rush someone if they have a Shotgun)
  • Whenever you get a chance to hit a leg or what ever may be exposed, shoot.
  • In order to win, learn the tricks of the map. Learn the most efficient places to plant C4 and where the best places to throw flashbangs are to ricochet off of the walls.
  • Camp, camp camp. As a newbie go ahead. As long as your not killing many players usually they will not ban you yet. As you get better though try to do this constantly but you still can. If both players camp then try to find a spot where they'll be willing to move so you have a fair chance at killing them.
  • If you are stuck against snipers, use the sniper rifle's poor reload time to your advantage and attack during the reloading period. This way, you can defeat even the sharpest of snipers.
  • If you are trying to move and shoot, you'll probably miss. If you want overall accuracy, stay still, click to fire, don't hold and fire. Instead click when your ready to aim and shoot. Sometimes if your opponent misses enough this is a great time to take your time and kill them.
  • Find a friend or someone who is not too skilled and get them to host. You should then practice your aiming on them. Be sure to try to do long shots, close up, headshots, and normal in as little shots as you can.
  • Try not to use SR. It has low bullets, long time reloading and you can only shoot every 2-3 seconds after you just shot. Only use SR60 if you are an experienced player that can use HS successfully.
  • Headshooting is what seperates the newbies from the pros.

    • Fair play

     In almost any game there will be players who will cheat/hack, team kill, curse or annoy. The question is what should you do or not to do. You should never try to sink to their level. If you do then your just as bad as them. If your hosting the game feel free to kick or ban them from your server. If someone is hacking or cheating please contact KingOfHeart. If KingOfHeart is not around then contact Kingson and give as much detail as possible. If you know how to cheat or hack then please report it and keep it to yourself. Certain cheats can spread fast if it's easy to do. If your just a regular player and you don't like what's happening in a game then either leave or deal with it. If someone is team killing everyone it's best not to team kill them back. Just try to get the hosts attention to get them removed or switch teams. If you kill back and the host is not paying much attention you'll be the one that ends up getting banned. Just follow this advice and you will be respected. If you follow none of these rules then one day you just might find yourself removed from the game.
